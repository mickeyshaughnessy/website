  • Recipe Amalgamation Made Simple.

    By scraping over 80,000 recipes from public cooking websites, Gabe Shaughnessy and I assembled a database of popular recipes. We built a custom NLP algorithm to process the recipe names, instructions and descriptions to come up with a so-called metarecipe, describing the essential ingredients for anything you'd like to cook. Is vanilla required for chocolate chip cookies? What the key ingredients in dog food? Is bread really required for bread pudding? Give it a shot and find out! (click the text).

  • Large Scale Atomistic Simulation of Carbon Nanotube Contacts.

    At Sandia National Labs I modeled carbon nanotube electronics using density functional theory and molecular dynamics on large-scale supercomputers. In this simulation, a carbon nanotube (purple atoms) is viewed head-on while resting on a silicon substrate surface (light blue atoms). Palladium and titanium atoms (silver and light green) form a metallic contact around the nanotube. Complex interactions at materials interfaces determine the effective contact resistance.

  • Machine Learning Applied to Multiscale Simulations.

    A technique for using cached results of quantum mechanical calculations to drive a multiscale simulation of the dynamics of a Si melt. Reese Jones and I implemented an efficient method to use a large scale database of atomic configurations using a k-nearest-neighbor approach and ball tree-like search method.   • Performance of a Market Maker Strategy.

    In the programming game Stockfighter, Kyle Lange and I developed a program to act as a market maker and trade with other participants. Using heuristics about price fluctuations and by quickly cancelling outstanding orders, the algorithm is able to consistently earn a profit in a competitive market under changing conditions. NAV is the net account value ($).
